# Articles

### Get started

- [Get started with
  tabular](https://vthanik.github.io/tabular/articles/tabular.md):

  Your first submission-grade clinical table in ten minutes: from a
  pre-summarised data frame to a live preview and five output formats.

- [Core
  concepts](https://vthanik.github.io/tabular/articles/core-concepts.md):

  The mental model the rest of the guide assumes: the wide-data
  contract, the anatomy of a clinical table page, the immutable verb
  pipeline, and why decimal alignment matters.

### Recipes

- [Columns &
  headers](https://vthanik.github.io/tabular/articles/columns-and-headers.md):

  The column surface: name and configure every column with cols() and
  col_spec(), then build multi-level header bands with headers().

- [Rows, grouping &
  pagination](https://vthanik.github.io/tabular/articles/rows-grouping-pagination.md):

  Control the order of display rows, partition a table into labelled
  subgroups, and govern how it splits across printed pages.

- [Styling](https://vthanik.github.io/tabular/articles/styling.md):

  One style() verb plus a cells\_\*() location drives every visual rule;
  presets carry the cosmetic defaults, and style templates package a
  reusable house style.

- [Clinical
  cookbook](https://vthanik.github.io/tabular/articles/clinical-cookbook.md):

  Six complete, submission-shaped tables built end to end: demographics,
  adverse events, vital signs, best overall response, and a subgrouped
  analysis. Copy a recipe and swap in your own summarised data.

### Advanced

- [From a cards
  ARD](https://vthanik.github.io/tabular/articles/from-ard.md):

  Bridge long-format Analysis Results Data into the wide frame tabular

- [Fonts &
  fidelity](https://vthanik.github.io/tabular/articles/fonts-and-fidelity.md):

  Why clinical tables are monospace, how decimal alignment depends on
  font metrics, auditing fonts with check_fonts(), and why the print

- [Architecture](https://vthanik.github.io/tabular/articles/architecture.md):

  How tabular works under the hood: the immutable spec, the six-phase
  resolve engine, backend dispatch, and the QC and audit artefacts.
